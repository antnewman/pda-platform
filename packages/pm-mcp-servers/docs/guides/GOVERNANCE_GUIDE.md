# Governance Guide for pda-platform Adoption

## Introduction to Governance Requirements

Implementing the pda-platform requires establishing clear governance structures and policies.

## Why Governance Matters

- Accountable decision-making supported by the platform
- Appropriate human oversight of algorithmic outputs
- Fair and consistent application across the organization
- Transparency with affected stakeholders
- Compliance with regulatory and policy requirements

## Key Roles and Responsibilities

### Senior Responsible Officer (SRO)
- Accountable for platform deployment and use
- Reviews and approves governance policies
- Responsible for escalation procedures

### Senior Information Risk Owner (SIRO)
- Owns information security and data protection aspects
- Reviews data handling and access controls
- Ensures GDPR compliance

### Platform Administrator
- Manages platform configuration and maintenance
- Controls access and user permissions
- Maintains audit logs and records

### Platform Users
- Input and validate project data
- Review and interpret platform outputs
- Document their use of platform results

### Review/Approval Authority
- Reviews platform outputs before high-stakes decisions
- Documents review and approval decisions
- Escalates concerning findings

### Compliance and Audit
- Monitors use of platform against governance policies
- Conducts periodic audits of platform usage
- Reviews for potential bias or unfair outcomes

## Policies to Establish

1. **Acceptable Use Policy** - What the platform may and may not be used for
2. **Data Quality Policy** - Standards for input data quality
3. **Output Review and Approval Policy** - Which outputs require human review
4. **Change Management Policy** - Process for updating platform configuration
5. **Data Protection and Security Policy** - Data handling requirements
6. **Performance and Monitoring Policy** - Metrics for monitoring system performance
7. **Training and Capability Policy** - Required training for all platform users

## Human Oversight Requirements

- For routine decisions: Project managers review outputs
- For resource allocation: Senior review authority approves recommendations
- For escalation: Portfolio manager reviews with senior leadership
- For governance decisions: Compliance and audit function reviews

## Documentation Requirements

Maintain system, decision, performance, and compliance documentation. Keep audit logs for minimum 12 months.

## Escalation Criteria

Escalate when:
- Output confidence is very low (less than 0.3)
- Results conflict significantly with user expectations
- Output suggests critical project problems
- Findings have potential regulatory implications
- Multiple data quality issues detected
- Stakeholder complaints about use
